Fiber Jack (ONT) Installation Guide

A deep dive into the placement, installation, and verification of all supported Optical Network Terminal (ONT) models.

Universal Placement Guidelines (All Models)

Before installing any model, ensure the chosen location meets these critical requirements.

  • Interior Wall Only: The ONT must be placed on an interior wall, never an exterior one.
  • Power Source: The location must be within 4 feet of a standard electrical outlet.
  • Height: The top of the wall plate should be 16-18 inches from the floor or aligned with other nearby outlets for a clean look.
  • Ventilation: Ensure a minimum of 2 inches of clearance around the device for proper airflow. It must be kept away from heat sources.
  • Prohibited Areas: Avoid installing in closets, cabinets, bathrooms, kitchens, or behind doors/furniture unless absolutely necessary and approved. Garages are also discouraged.

ONT Models: GPON vs. XGS-PON

Standard GPON ONT

Used for plans 2 Gig and under in non-XGS areas.

Models: GOGP220C, GOGP222C, GOGP220H

Installation Process

  • Light Test: Use a light meter on the 1490nm wavelength setting.
  • Drilling & Mounting: Drill hole from NIU, run fiber patch cable, and mount the Fiber Jack securely to a mud ring.
  • Connection: Clean the fiber connector and plug it securely into the ONT's optical port.
  • Power: Connect the 12V ⎓ 0.5A power adapter and turn on.

10-Gig XGS-PON ONT

Required for all multi-gig plans (5G, 8G) and all installs in new markets.

Models: GOXP330C, GOXP330H

Hardware Alert: This ONT is larger (~4.25"W x 8.5"L) and requires a 12.0V ⎓ 1.0A power adapter. Do not use older, lower-wattage adapters.

Installation Process

  • Light Test: Use a light meter on the 1577nm wavelength setting.
  • Mounting Bracket: Secure the mounting bracket to the wall/mud ring. Loop the fiber around the bracket's built-in slack management.
  • Connection: Fit the ONT onto the bracket and slide down until you hear an audible "click", confirming the fiber is engaged.
  • Ethernet: Connect a CAT6A cable from the ONT's "Network" port to the router.

Reset Button Functions

  • Soft Reset (1 sec press): Reboots the CPU without losing configuration.
  • Hard Reset (8+ sec press): Clears the ONT's configuration from the OLT and reboots.

Special Cases & Procedures

Centralized ONT / "Fiber Jack Farm"

In some MDU (apartment/condo) properties, ONTs are not located in each unit but are centralized in a utility closet (IDF/MDF room).

  • Customer Connection: The customer will have an Ethernet wall jack in their unit, not a Fiber Jack. The router plugs into this jack.
  • Technician Access: You must gain access to the IDF/MDF room to service the ONT. Check Salesforce/BSS for access notes or contact the building manager.
  • Identification: If the jacks in the closet are unlabeled, you may need to use a toner on the customer's in-unit Ethernet cable to identify the correct port connected to their ONT.

Verification & Troubleshooting

Always test light levels at the NIU first. If the NIU reading is good but the reading at the ONT location is bad, the in-home fiber patch cable is the likely culprit.

Fiber Cleaning Best Practices

Dirty connections are a primary cause of light level issues. Proper cleaning is non-negotiable.

  • Clean Both Sides: Always clean both the fiber connector end-face and the inside of the bulkhead/port it's connecting to.
  • Clean Every Time: Even brand new patch cords with dust caps must be cleaned before first use.
  • Use the Right Tool:
    • Click Cleaner: Good for both connectors and inside bulkheads. 1-2 clicks is sufficient.
    • CLETOP Cleaner: For connectors only. Wipe, rotate 90°, wipe again.
    • Wet Cleaning: Use pre-wet wipes/swabs for stubborn dirt, but always follow with a dry clean to remove residue.
  • Verify After Cleaning: Re-test the light level after cleaning to confirm improvement.
