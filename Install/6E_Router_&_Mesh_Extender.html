Wi-Fi 6E Router & Mesh Extender Installation

A complete guide to installing, configuring, and verifying the GR6EXX0C Router and GE6E220C Mesh Extender.

Universal Wi-Fi Best Practices

  • Single SSID is Mandatory: For the best customer experience, the network must be configured with a single, unified Wi-Fi name (SSID). Do not split the 2.4, 5, and 6 GHz bands. This allows devices to seamlessly roam to the optimal frequency.
  • Placement is Crucial: Proper equipment placement is key to performance. Use a Wi-Fi analyzer app (like NetSpot) to find the best locations, avoiding closets, cabinets, and obstructions.
  • Set Expectations: Clearly explain to the customer that maximum speeds are only achieved on a hardwired connection and that Wi-Fi speeds will vary based on device capability, distance, and home construction.

Device Installation & Configuration

Wi-Fi 6E Router (GR6EXX0C)

Specifications

Wi-Fi StandardTri-band Wi-Fi 6E (802.11ax)
Ports1x 10G WAN (Silver), 3x 2.5G LAN
RangeUp to 2,000 sq. ft.
SecurityWPA3

Placement Guidelines

  • Place in a central location within the home.
  • Position in an open space (e.g., shelf, TV stand), not an enclosed cabinet or closet.
  • Place in the room where Wi-Fi is used most often.
  • Ensure it's in a well-ventilated area with at least 2" of clearance.

Installation & Configuration Steps

  1. Connect a CAT6A Ethernet cable from the Fiber Jack's LAN port to the silver 10G WAN port on the router.
  2. Connect the power adapter and wait ~3 minutes for the Power and WAN/Internet LEDs to turn Solid Green.
  3. Guide the customer to log into their GFiber Portal or Mobile App.
  4. In the "Network Settings," have the customer create a Wi-Fi Name (SSID) and Password.
  5. Once saved, wait ~60 seconds for the router's Wi-Fi LED to turn Solid Green. The network is now active.

Wi-Fi 6E Mesh Extender (GE6E220C)

Install Only When Necessary! Extenders are for homes larger than ~2,000 sq. ft. or with confirmed dead spots. Unneeded extenders can degrade Wi-Fi performance.

Specifications

BackhaulDedicated 4x4 6GHz wireless
Ports1x 2.5G LAN, 1x 1G LAN
CompatibilityOnly pairs with GR6EXX0C router.

Placement Guidelines

  • Place the extender roughly halfway between the router and the furthest area that needs coverage.
  • Do not place it in the dead zone itself; it needs a strong signal from the router.
  • Keep it in an open area, within the 6GHz coverage of the main router for a strong backhaul connection.

Pairing Instructions (Wireless)

  1. Ensure the router's Wi-Fi is already set up.
  2. Power on the extender within 3-6 feet of the router. Wait for its LED to turn Solid White.
  3. Press the WPS button on the back of the router.
  4. Within 2 minutes, press the WPS button on the base of the extender.
  5. Wait 4-6 minutes. The extender LED will blink white, then turn Solid Green upon success.
  6. Move the extender to its final location.

Pairing Instructions (Wired - Preferred)

  1. Connect a CAT6A Ethernet cable from a LAN port on the router to the 2.5G port on the extender.
  2. Power on the extender. It will automatically pair and the LED will turn Solid Green within 5-6 minutes.

Verification & Troubleshooting

Verification & Speed Testing

RSSI Testing (Wi-Fi Health)

Use a tool like NetSpot or check Salesforce/BSS to measure the extender's backhaul signal strength (RSSI). Aim for the "Recommended" range.

RSSI ValueDefinitionAction
> -50 dBmToo CloseMove extender further from router.
-50 to -66 dBmRecommendedOptimal placement. No action needed.
< -66 dBmToo FarMove extender closer to router.

Hardline Speed Test

  • For multi-gig plans, you must use the 10G silver port on the router to test maximum speeds.
  • Connect your laptop using a CAT6A cable.
  • Use the Ookla SpeedTest Desktop App for all installation speed tests.

Common Troubleshooting

Extender is Offline or Not Responding

  • First, verify it is plugged in and within range of the router.
  • If it needs to be re-paired, follow the pairing instructions.
  • For a full reset, use a paperclip to press the Reset button on the base for at least 10 seconds. This will restore factory defaults, and the extender must be paired again.

Firmware 1.5.4+ Changes

  • Factory Reset: If you factory reset the Router, you MUST also factory reset any paired Extenders. They will need to be re-paired from scratch.
  • Backhaul Loss: If the extender loses its connection to the router, its Wi-Fi signal (fronthaul) will now automatically turn off. This accurately shows the customer there is a problem. The Wi-Fi will re-enable once the backhaul connection is restored.
