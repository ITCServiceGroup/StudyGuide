Comprehensive GFiber Installation Process

A detailed, step-by-step guide to the entire installation appointment, from preparation to close-out.

1. Preparation & Pre-Call

Setting Up for Success

Daily Prep Checklist

  • Check your schedule for the day in Salesforce/BSS and activate your route.
  • Grab the designated Customer Premise Equipment (CPE) for your jobs from the warehouse. Ensure bags are checked for accuracy.
  • Check out and inspect your vehicle, tools, and kits using the inventory management tool.
  • Confirm you have all required materials and safety equipment for the day's work.

The Pre-Call (15+ mins before arrival)

  • Call your first customer within 15 minutes of your arrival window (Note: 8am appointments should be contacted no earlier than 7:30am).
    • Greet the customer and verify the appointment time.
    • Verify the address and confirm any access instructions (gate codes, etc.).
    • Verify the customer's service plan.
    • Confirm an adult (18+) will be present for the entire installation.
    • Ask about any pets in the home.
    • Ask the customer to have their GFiber account login and password ready for Wi-Fi setup.
  • Once you are enroute, activate your route in your field service tool and select the On My Way Button to notify the customer.

2. Arrival, Greeting, and Assessment

Planning the Perfect Install

Arrival & Initial Contact

  • Arrive within 10 minutes of the scheduled time. Park legally and set up safety cones.
  • Access your tools, check into the job, and review the work order.
  • At the door, ensure your badge is visible, introduce yourself, and state your purpose.

NIU Light Test & Assessment

  • Inform the customer you'll start by accessing the gray box on the side of the house, the Network Interface Unit (NIU).
  • At the NIU, use a fiber light meter to test the signal.
    • The meter's test channel must be set to 1490nm for GPON or 1577nm for XGS-PON.
    • The acceptable optical power level is between -9.0 dBm and -23.0 dBm.
  • Clean the fiber jumper before inserting and testing. Write the date and final light level on the inside lid of the NIU with a permanent marker.
  • Note the light level in the installation notes in Salesforce/BSS.

Hardware Consultation & Home Survey

  • Put on boot covers. Explain the function of each piece of equipment you will install.
  • Perform a walk-through survey with the customer to finalize the installation plan. Ask key questions:
    • "Where in the home do you use Wi-Fi the most?"
    • "Do you have other services like satellite or a home phone we need to be aware of?"
    • "Do you use the Google Home App?" (for applicable installs)
    • "May I have your permission to check areas like closets or the garage if they provide the best wiring path?"
  • Identify the best locations for the Fiber Jack and Router, then assess and discuss the residential gateway placement with the customer.
  • Identify any computers/devices that will need to be hardwired.

3. Wiring and Hardware Installation

Building the Network

Wiring and Fiber Jack Installation

  • Run the fiber patch cable from the NIU to the designated Fiber Jack location, using discreet paths and securing the cable every 18 inches. Check for pinches.
  • If installing into drywall, install a mud ring at 16" from the floor or aligned with other plates.
  • Clean the fiber patch cable connector before connecting to the Fiber Jack.
  • Mount the Fiber Jack. Scan its serial number into Salesforce/BSS and note its location (e.g., Living Room).
  • Plug the Fiber Jack into a power outlet and turn it on.

Handling an Existing Fiber Jack: If a functional Fiber Jack already exists, it can be reused. If it needs to be moved and the existing fiber is too short, use a coupler to extend it. Be careful not to damage the existing fiber.

Router and Mesh Extender Setup

  • Run a CAT6A Ethernet cable from the Fiber Jack's LAN port to the 10G silver WAN port on the back of the Wi-Fi 6E Router.
  • Install devices on the account in Salesforce/BSS. Power on the router and wait for the Power and WAN LEDs to turn solid green (this can take up to 5 minutes).
  • Mesh Extender Setup (if needed):
    • The customer's Wi-Fi SSID and password must be set up on the router first before pairing.
    • For wireless pairing, press the WPS button on the router, then the extender. Wait for the extender's light to turn solid green.
    • For wired backhaul (preferred), connect an Ethernet cable from a router LAN port to the extender's WAN port.

4. Service Demonstration & Education

Verifying and Empowering

Internet Demo with Customer

  • Ask the customer to log into their GFiber account. Give them an overview of the account dashboard.
  • Confirm their account is set up for automatic billing and show them where they can manage their network settings.
  • Perform a speed test using the Ookla SpeedTest Desktop App on a hardwired connection to show the plan's maximum speed.
  • Run a second test on their Wi-Fi device and explain the expected differences.

Fiber Phone Demo (If Applicable)

  • Verify any services that need to run on the phone line (e.g., alarm, medical alert).
  • Connect the Phone Box to the router's LAN port and power it on.
  • Test for a dial tone. Test both incoming and outgoing calls.
  • Crucially, test Emergency (911) to verify the call connects and the address is received correctly.
  • Show the customer the setup instructions for voicemail and other features.

Video Service Demo (If Applicable)

  • If requested, guide the customer through signing into their chosen streaming service (e.g., YouTube TV, Sling TV) on at least one device.
  • Let the customer know that free trials may be available and that they will be billed directly by the streaming provider, not GFiber.
  • Guide them through the Search, Browse, and DVR features of the service.

5. Clean Up & Close Out

Finishing Professionally

Final Steps Checklist

  • Clean up all work locations. Remove all debris, wire clippings, dust, and packaging. Use the company-issued vacuum; never the customer's.
  • Leave your business card and any relevant marketing or help-center collateral.
  • Answer any final questions and remind the customer about the satisfaction survey they will receive via email.
  • Conduct a final check for all your bags and tools before leaving.
  • Complete the job by closing out the ticket in Salesforce/BSS with accurate and detailed notes. This is a mandatory final step.
