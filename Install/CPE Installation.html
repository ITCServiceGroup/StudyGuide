Definitive Hardware Installation Guide

The complete, unabridged guide for installing the Fiber Jack (ONT), Wi-Fi 6E Router, and Mesh Extender system.

Universal Installation Guidelines

Hardware Placement Rules

  • Interior Wall Only: All hardware must be placed on an interior wall.
  • Power Source: Location must be within 4 feet of an electrical outlet.
  • Height: Wall plates should be 16-18 inches from the floor or aligned with other outlets.
  • Ventilation: Ensure a minimum of 2 inches of clearance around all devices.
  • Prohibited Areas: Avoid closets, cabinets, bathrooms, kitchens, and behind doors or furniture.

Wi-Fi Best Practices

  • Single SSID is Mandatory: Configure the network with one unified Wi-Fi name. Do not split the 2.4/5/6 GHz bands.
  • Placement is Crucial: Use a Wi-Fi analyzer app (like NetSpot) to find optimal, open locations for the router and mesh extenders.
  • Set Expectations: Explain to the customer that maximum speeds are achieved on a hardwired connection.

Step 1: Fiber Jack (ONT) Installation

ONT Models: GPON vs. XGS-PON

Standard GPON ONT

Models: GOGP220C, GOGP222C, GOGP220H

Used for plans 2 Gig and under in non-XGS areas.

Installation Process

  • Light Test: Use a light meter on the 1490nm wavelength setting.
  • Drilling & Mounting: Drill hole from NIU, run fiber patch cable, and mount the Fiber Jack securely to a mud ring.
  • Connection: Clean the fiber connector and plug it securely into the ONT's optical port.
  • Power: Connect the 12V ⎓ 0.5A power adapter and turn on.

10-Gig XGS-PON ONT

Models: GOXP330C, GOXP330H

Required for all multi-gig plans (5G, 8G) and all installs in new markets.

Hardware Alert: This ONT is larger (~4.25"W x 8.5"L) and requires a 12.0V ⎓ 1.0A power adapter. Do not use older, lower-wattage adapters.

Installation Process

  • Light Test: Use a light meter on the 1577nm wavelength setting.
  • Mounting Bracket: Secure the mounting bracket to the wall/mud ring. Loop the fiber around the bracket's built-in slack management.
  • Connection: Fit the ONT onto the bracket and slide down until you hear an audible "click", confirming the fiber is engaged.
  • Ethernet: Connect a CAT6A cable from the ONT's "Network" port to the router.

Reset Button Functions

  • Soft Reset (1 sec press): Reboots the CPU without losing configuration.
  • Hard Reset (8+ sec press): Clears the ONT's configuration from the OLT and reboots.

Special Cases: Centralized ONT / "Fiber Jack Farm"

  • Customer Connection: The customer will have an Ethernet wall jack in their unit. The router plugs into this jack.
  • Technician Access: You must gain access to the building's utility closet (IDF/MDF room) to service the ONT.
  • Identification: If jacks are unlabeled, use a toner on the customer's in-unit Ethernet cable to identify the correct port.

Step 2: Router Installation

GFiber Wi-Fi 6E Router (GR6EXX0C)

Specifications

Wi-Fi StandardTri-band Wi-Fi 6E (802.11ax)
Ports1x 10G WAN (Silver), 3x 2.5G LAN
RangeUp to 2,000 sq. ft.
SecurityWPA3

Placement Guidelines

  • Place in a central location within the home.
  • Position in an open space (e.g., shelf, TV stand), not an enclosed cabinet or closet.
  • Place in the room where Wi-Fi is used most often.
  • Ensure it's in a well-ventilated area with at least 2" of clearance.

Installation & Configuration Steps

  1. Connect to ONT: Run a CAT6A Ethernet cable from the Fiber Jack's LAN port to the silver 10G WAN port on the router.
  2. Power On: Connect the power adapter and wait ~3 minutes for the Power and WAN/Internet LEDs to turn Solid Green.
  3. Configure Wi-Fi: Guide the customer to log into their GFiber Portal or Mobile App.
  4. Set Network Credentials: In the "Network Settings," have the customer create a Wi-Fi Name (SSID) and Password.
  5. Confirm Network is Active: Once saved, wait ~60 seconds for the router's Wi-Fi LED to turn Solid Green.

Bring Your Own Router (BYOR)

Installation Policy: Even if a customer plans to use their own router, the GFiber Wi-Fi 6E Router must be installed and activated first for all Lifestyle Product plans. The customer can then plug their own router into one of the LAN ports of the GFiber router.

Customer Router Minimum Specifications

  • Wi-Fi Standard: Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) with Dual-band or Tri-band capabilities.
  • Security: WPA2 security protocols (WPA3 recommended).
  • Ports (Multi-Gig Plans): To achieve speeds over 1 Gig, the customer's router must have a multi-gig WAN port (e.g., 2.5G, 5G, or 10GBASE-T). A standard 1-Gig WAN port will be a bottleneck.
  • Incompatible Devices: A cable or DSL modem-router combo unit will not work with GFiber service.

Step 3: Wi-Fi 6E Mesh Extender (GE6E220C) Installation

Install Only When Necessary! Unneeded extenders can degrade Wi-Fi performance. They are for homes larger than ~2,000 sq. ft. or with confirmed dead spots.

Specifications & Placement

BackhaulDedicated 4x4 6GHz wireless
Ports1x 2.5G LAN, 1x 1G LAN
CompatibilityOnly pairs with GR6EXX0C router.

Placement Guidelines

  • Place the extender roughly halfway between the router and the furthest area that needs coverage.
  • Do not place it in the dead zone itself; it needs a strong signal from the router.
  • Keep it in an open area, within the 6GHz coverage of the main router.

Pairing Instructions

Wireless Pairing

  1. Prerequisite: The router's Wi-Fi must be set up first.
  2. Initial Pairing: Power on the extender within 3-6 feet of the router. Wait for its LED to turn Solid White.
  3. Press WPS: Press the WPS button on the router, then on the extender.
  4. Wait: Pairing can take 4-6 minutes. Success is indicated by a Solid Green LED.
  5. Final Placement: Move the extender to its final location.

Wired Pairing (Preferred)

  1. Connect Cable: Run a CAT6A Ethernet cable from a LAN port on the router to the 2.5G port on the extender.
  2. Power On: Power on the extender. The LED will turn Solid Green within 5-6 minutes.

Common Troubleshooting Steps

RSSI Testing (Extender Health)

Measure the extender's backhaul signal. Aim for the "Recommended" range.

RSSI ValueDefinitionAction
> -50 dBmToo CloseMove extender further from router.
-50 to -66 dBmRecommendedOptimal placement. No action needed.
< -66 dBmToo FarMove extender closer to router.

Hardline Speed Test

  • For multi-gig plans, you must use the 10G silver port on the router.
  • Connect your laptop using a CAT6A cable.
  • Use the Ookla SpeedTest Desktop App for all installation speed tests.

Firmware 1.5.4+ Changes

  • Factory Reset: If you factory reset the Router, you MUST also factory reset any paired Extenders.
  • Backhaul Loss: If an extender loses its connection to the router, its Wi-Fi signal will now automatically turn off.
